Most of my projects start as something I wished already existed. A homelab I can
run entirely from Git. A set of agents that diagnose and repair a Kubernetes
cluster on their own. A small controller that only rents cloud servers while the
cluster is actually under load, then shuts them off again. A pile of Raspberry Pi
sensors keeping an eye on the weather and local wildlife. My bachelor thesis came
out of the same itch, getting LLM agents to fix a cluster without making things
worse. When I'm not at the keyboard I'm usually tinkering with robotics and IoT
with the 3D printer running, or reading, mostly a mix of systems books and fiction.
